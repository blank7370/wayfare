The whole point

A cap isn't a marketing trick. It's the product.

A

Tables that fit everyone

Sixteen is the largest group a single guide can actually know by name, seat at one table, and move through a border without losing an hour. Past that, you're a tour. We don't run tours.

B

Rooms we can promise

Small lodges and family guesthouses can't absorb forty beds on a date. Capping the group is what lets us book the good rooms the ones that don't exist at scale.

C

Scarcity that's real

When a counter says two seats, there are two. We don't reopen sold-out dates or quietly add a row. The limit on the screen is the limit on the ground.

Booking a seat

Four steps, no sales call

  1. 01

    Pick a date that's open

    Each route runs a handful of fixed departures a year. Find one with seats left on the board above.

  2. 02

    Claim your seats

    Reserve one to four seats. The counter drops the moment you confirm your spot is held, not negotiated.

  3. 03

    We sort the bundle

    Flights between stops, lodging, guide, transfers, permits. One itinerary lands in your inbox.

  4. 04

    Show up with a bag

    You arrive at the meeting point. Everything from there is already arranged and paid.
